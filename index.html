Second Degree Equations

Quadratic Equation: ax 2 + bx + c

Steps for finding roots of the 2nd degree equation

First you have to find the Discriminant of the equation i.e. D

Now D = b2 - 4*a*c

1. b2 −4ac <0 There are no real roots.

2. b2 −4ac = 0 There is one real root.

3. b2 −4ac > 0 There are two real roots.
